This is an educational quishing (QR-phishing) demo by the LIU Cybersecurity Club.
Controlled demonstration.
If this were real, you could be redirected to a fake login, pushed to download malware, or tricked into handing over your credentials.

What attackers count on:

  • Blind trust in stickers, posters, or table placards.
  • No preview of the destination link before tapping.
  • Signing in on a page that only looks legitimate.
Protect yourself next time
  1. Preview the URL (camera shows it) and read the full domain carefully.
  2. Watch for typosquatting (liυ.edu vs liu.edu, weird subdomains, extra dashes).
  3. Don’t sign in from a QR page—open the trusted site manually/bookmark.
  4. Use MFA and a password manager to limit damage if you slip.
  5. Report suspicious QR codes to organizers or campus IT.
